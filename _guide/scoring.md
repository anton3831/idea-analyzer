# Scoring Guide

## Purpose

Produce a consistent, justified score for each business idea across key dimensions.
Scores must reflect evidence, not intuition. Every score below 7 requires a specific reason.
Every score above 8 requires specific evidence.

---

## Scoring Scale (Per Dimension)

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1–2 | Critically weak. Fundamental flaw. Must fix before proceeding. |
| 3–4 | Weak. Significant gaps or risks that undermine viability. |
| 5–6 | Moderate. Functional but lacks clear advantage or has unresolved risks. |
| 7–8 | Strong. Solid foundation with minor gaps that can be addressed. |
| 9–10 | Exceptional. Evidence-backed strength with clear competitive advantage. |

---

## Scoring Dimensions

### Dimension 1 — Market Opportunity (Weight: 25%)

**What it measures**: Size, growth rate, and accessibility of the target market.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | TAM >$1B, growing >20%/yr, proven demand signals, clear customer segment |
| 7–8 | TAM >$100M, moderate growth, some demand signals |
| 5–6 | TAM $10M–$100M, or unclear growth, weak demand signals |
| 3–4 | TAM <$10M, or declining market, or manufactured demand |
| 1–2 | No identifiable market, no clear customer |

---

### Dimension 2 — Business Model Viability (Weight: 25%)

**What it measures**: Revenue potential, margin structure, and monetization clarity.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Clear recurring revenue, LTV/CAC >3x, gross margin >70%, early monetization possible |
| 7–8 | Viable revenue model, reasonable unit economics, some monetization risk |
| 5–6 | Revenue model exists but has margin or scale issues |
| 3–4 | Unclear how to make money, low margins, high CAC |
| 1–2 | No viable path to revenue |

---

### Dimension 3 — Competitive Differentiation (Weight: 20%)

**What it measures**: Uniqueness and defensibility of the value proposition.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Proprietary data, network effects, or technology moat. Clear gap in the market. |
| 7–8 | Meaningful differentiation (focus, UX, price, speed) that is hard to replicate quickly |
| 5–6 | Some differentiation but easily copied within 6–12 months |
| 3–4 | "Better UX" or minor feature difference only. No structural advantage. |
| 1–2 | Exact copy of existing solution with no differentiation |

---

### Dimension 4 — Execution Feasibility (Weight: 15%)

**What it measures**: How realistic it is for this team to build and launch this product.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Team has deep domain expertise, all critical skills present, clear GTM plan |
| 7–8 | Mostly capable team, minor gaps fillable with early hires or advisors |
| 5–6 | Capable team but missing a critical skill (tech, sales, or domain) |
| 3–4 | Major skill gaps, no clear GTM, or execution is highly capital-intensive |
| 1–2 | Team fundamentally mismatched with the problem |

---

### Dimension 5 — Risk Profile (Weight: 15%)

**What it measures**: Severity and number of risks that could kill the business.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | No Critical risks, few Medium risks, well-understood risk landscape |
| 7–8 | No Critical risks, some High risks with mitigation plans |
| 5–6 | One Critical risk or multiple High risks without clear mitigation |
| 3–4 | Multiple Critical risks or systemic structural risk |
| 1–2 | Existential risk (regulatory, legal, or market) with no mitigation path |

---

## Composite Score Calculation

```
Composite Score = 
  (Market × 0.25) + 
  (Business Model × 0.25) + 
  (Differentiation × 0.20) + 
  (Execution × 0.15) + 
  (Risk × 0.15)
```

---

## Final Verdict Thresholds

| Composite Score | Verdict |
|-----------------|---------|
| 8.0 – 10.0 | **Go** — Strong idea, proceed to build or fundraise |
| 6.0 – 7.9 | **Pivot** — Core has promise but requires specific improvements before proceeding |
| 4.0 – 5.9 | **Major Rework** — Fundamental issues; do not build until resolved |
| 1.0 – 3.9 | **Drop** — Not viable in current form |

---

## Justification Rules

- **Every score must have a 1-sentence justification.**
- Scores of 9 or 10 require specific evidence (data point, competitor example, customer validation).
- Scores of 1 or 2 require a named specific flaw, not a generic statement.
- If data is insufficient to score accurately, use 5 as default and add: *(Insufficient data — assumption used)*.

---

## Output Format

```
### Scoring

| Dimension | Score | Justification |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Market Opportunity | [X]/10 | [1-sentence reason] |
| Business Model Viability | [X]/10 | [1-sentence reason] |
| Competitive Differentiation | [X]/10 | [1-sentence reason] |
| Execution Feasibility | [X]/10 | [1-sentence reason] |
| Risk Profile | [X]/10 | [1-sentence reason] |

**Composite Score**: [X.X] / 10

**Final Verdict**: Go / Pivot / Major Rework / Drop

**Verdict Reason**: [2–3 sentences explaining the verdict with reference to the highest-impact dimension]
```
