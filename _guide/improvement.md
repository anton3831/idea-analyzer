# Improvement Guide

## Purpose

Transform weak or unfocused ideas into sharper, more viable ones.
Do not just list problems — provide concrete direction on how to fix them.

---

## When to Apply This Guide

Apply improvement logic when:
- Market is weak or too broad
- Business model has low margins or unclear monetization
- Risk assessment reveals High or Critical issues
- The idea is a "me too" copy of existing solutions with no differentiation
- The target customer is vague ("everyone", "all businesses", "millennials")

---

## Strategy 1 — Narrowing (Niche Down)

**Problem it solves**: Idea is too broad to compete effectively or too expensive to market.

**Rule**: A focused product for a specific segment beats a generic product for everyone.

### How to Apply

1. Take the current target customer and add 2–3 more constraints:
   - Industry vertical (e.g., "freelancers" → "freelance UX designers")
   - Geography (e.g., "small businesses" → "small businesses in Southeast Asia")
   - Behavior (e.g., "remote workers" → "remote workers in their first remote job")

2. Re-evaluate the value proposition against the narrowed segment:
   - Does the problem still exist for this group?
   - Is it MORE painful for this group than the broad audience?
   - Can you reach them efficiently? (community, platform, search terms)

3. Validate the niche is large enough:
   - Minimum viable niche: ~10,000 potential customers at a price that generates $1M+ ARR.

**Example**:
- Too broad: "AI writing tool for content creators"
- Narrowed: "AI writing tool for B2B SaaS companies writing case studies"

---

## Strategy 2 — Positioning (Own a Space)

**Problem it solves**: Idea exists in a crowded market with no clear reason to choose this product over alternatives.

**Rule**: You don't need to be the best in the world — you need to be the obvious choice for a specific buyer.

### How to Apply

1. List the top 3–5 competitors in the space.
2. Map them on two axes (choose the axes that matter most in this market):
   - Examples: Price vs. Features, Ease of use vs. Power, Speed vs. Accuracy
3. Identify a gap in the map — where no competitor sits.
4. Define a positioning statement:

```
For [specific customer],
who [has specific problem],
[Product name] is the [category]
that [key differentiator].
Unlike [main competitor],
we [specific contrast].
```

**Rules:**
- Never position against everyone — pick one primary competitor to contrast against.
- Positioning must be defensible: based on a real structural advantage (cost, speed, focus, data, brand).
- "Better UX" is not a positioning — it is a feature claim anyone can make.

---

## Strategy 3 — Problem Reframing

**Problem it solves**: The idea is solving a symptom, not the root cause, or solving a problem customers don't actively feel.

**Rule**: Customers pay to remove pain, not to gain abstract value.

### How to Apply

1. Ask "Why does this problem exist?" 5 times (5-Whys method).
2. Identify the root cause.
3. Check: Is the root cause more painful than the symptom? If yes, solve that instead.
4. Test whether the reframed problem has clearer demand signals (are people paying for it already?).

**Example**:
- Original: "Tool to help managers give better performance reviews"
- After reframing: "The root problem is that managers don't have data on what their reports did all year"
- Pivot: "Automated work log that turns daily activity into quarterly performance summaries"

---

## Strategy 4 — Beachhead Market (Start Small, Expand)

**Problem it solves**: The full market is too large and competitive to enter directly.

**Rule**: Win one small market completely before expanding. First customers = your proof of concept and marketing.

### How to Apply

1. Identify the most reachable, most painful segment within the broader target market.
2. Define what "winning" that segment looks like (revenue target, customer count, NPS).
3. Design the product exclusively for that beachhead — don't build for the broader market yet.
4. Define the expansion path: once the beachhead is won, which adjacent segment is next?

---

## Output Format

```
### Improvement Suggestions

**Primary Issue**: [what is weak about the current idea]

**Recommended Strategy**: Narrowing / Positioning / Reframing / Beachhead
**Why This Strategy**: [1-sentence reason]

**Improved Idea (Revised Version)**:
- Target Customer: [narrowed description]
- Problem: [sharpened problem statement]
- Value Proposition: [clear, specific]
- Positioning Statement: [fill in the template above]

**Beachhead Market** (if applicable):
- First Target: [specific segment]
- Win Condition: [metric to hit before expanding]
- Expansion Path: [segment 2, segment 3]

**Next Validation Step**: [single most important experiment to run this week]
```
