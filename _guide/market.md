# Market Analysis Guide

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a real, accessible market exists for the business idea.
Do not accept vague market descriptions. Force specificity.

---

## Step 1 — Define the Market

Before estimating size, answer these questions:

- **Who exactly is the customer?** (age, job, behavior, geography)
- **What problem are they paying to solve today?** (existing solutions = existing spend)
- **What triggers the purchase decision?** (event, pain point, frustration)

If the customer cannot be described in 2 sentences, the idea lacks focus. Flag this as a critical gap.

---

## Step 2 — Market Size Estimation (TAM / SAM / SOM)

Use the following framework:

| Term | Definition | How to Estimate |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | All potential customers globally | Use industry reports, population data, or proxy markets |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Customers reachable with this business model | Filter TAM by geography, segment, and go-to-market reach |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic capture in Year 1–3 | Apply realistic market share % based on team and resources |

**Rules:**
- Always cite the basis for estimation (e.g., "based on X million freelancers in the US").
- Flag if TAM < $100M — typically too small for venture-scale; may still work for bootstrapped businesses.
- Flag if SOM > 5% of SAM in Year 1 — likely over-optimistic.

---

## Step 3 — Demand Evaluation

Assess whether demand is real, growing, or manufactured:

- **Proven demand**: Customers are currently paying for a similar solution (name them).
- **Latent demand**: Customers have the problem but no solution exists yet (requires validation).
- **Manufactured demand**: The product creates a new behavior (hardest to build — requires heavy marketing).

Signal checklist:
- [ ] Are there existing competitors making revenue? (proves demand)
- [ ] Are there active online communities around this problem? (Reddit, forums, Facebook groups)
- [ ] Are people searching for solutions? (Google Trends, keyword volume)
- [ ] Have any surveys or interviews been conducted with target users?

---

## Step 4 — Trend Identification

Evaluate whether the market is expanding or contracting:

- **Tailwind trends**: Regulatory changes, technology shifts, demographic changes that push demand UP.
  - Example: Remote work trend → increased demand for async communication tools.
- **Headwind trends**: Forces that work AGAINST the idea.
  - Example: Declining birth rate → headwind for baby product businesses.

Sources to reference:
- Google Trends (search volume over time)
- Industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey, CB Insights)
- Recent funding patterns in the space (high VC activity = hot market signal)

---

## Output Format

```
### Market Analysis

**Target Customer**: [1–2 sentence description]

**TAM**: $[X]B — [basis for estimate]
**SAM**: $[X]M — [filter applied]
**SOM (Year 1–3)**: $[X]M — [assumption used]

**Demand Type**: Proven / Latent / Manufactured
**Demand Signals**: [list 2–3 signals found or absent]

**Market Trend**: Growing / Flat / Declining
**Key Tailwinds**: [list]
**Key Headwinds**: [list]

**Market Verdict**: Strong / Moderate / Weak — [1-sentence reason]
```
